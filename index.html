Mathematical Career

I recieved my Bachelor's at MSU Denver and my Master's at University of British Columbia in Applied Mathematics. My master thesis was in dynamical systems and more specifically on the study of non-smooth slowly varying tipping points where I applied this to the Stommel model for ocean circulation. The implications could help better understand our own weather patterns and help us to prepare for abrupt changes in the governing dynamics.
While pursuing my interests, I found myself analyzing spatio-temporal trends with Groundwork Denver, discovering hidden relations signaling failure inside mining truck with SSR Mining, and even understanding the causes of child vulnerability with Children's Partnership of Surrey.
Due to the variety of projects, I have gained a broad understanding of mathematics and uses we have for them. I am always looking to use this ability to help and would love to consider a project if you have an idea! Please feel free to reach out to my email below.
